## Job Description (User-Provided)
Job Summary:
The HR Analyst plays a critical role in supporting data-driven decision-making within the Human Resources department. This position involves collecting, analyzing, and interpreting HR data to improve workforce planning, talent management, compliance, and operational efficiency. The HR Analyst provides insights through reports, dashboards, and recommendations to enhance HR strategies and business outcomes.

Key Responsibilities:
Data Analysis & Reporting:
Collect, clean, and analyze HR data (e.g., recruitment, turnover, compensation, performance, engagement).

Develop and maintain HR dashboards, metrics, and KPIs for leadership.

Generate regular and ad-hoc reports on workforce trends, diversity, retention, and other HR areas.

Identify data discrepancies and ensure accuracy in HR systems.

HR Systems & Process Improvement:
Support HRIS (Human Resources Information System) maintenance and optimization.

Assist in automating HR processes to improve efficiency.

Conduct audits to ensure data integrity and compliance with policies.

Workforce Planning & Talent Analytics:
Analyze hiring trends, time-to-fill, and recruitment sources to optimize talent acquisition.

Assess employee turnover and retention risks, providing actionable insights.

Support compensation benchmarking and pay equity analysis.

Compliance & Benchmarking:
Ensure HR practices comply with labor laws and regulations.

Conduct market research and benchmarking on HR best practices.

Assist in preparing reports for internal and external audits.

Strategic Support:
Collaborate with HR Business Partners and leadership to align HR initiatives with business goals.

Provide data-backed recommendations for policy improvements.

Support HR projects (e.g., employee engagement surveys, performance management).

Qualifications & Skills:
Education: Bachelor’s degree in Human Resources, Business Administration, Data Analytics, or related field.

Experience: 2+ years in HR analytics, data analysis, or a similar role.

Technical Skills:

Proficiency in HRIS (e.g., Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM).

Advanced Excel (pivot tables, VLOOKUP) and data visualization tools (Power BI, Tableau).

Familiarity with statistical analysis tools (R, Python, SPSS) is a plus.

Soft Skills:

Strong analytical and problem-solving abilities.

Attention to detail and ability to present complex data clearly.

Excellent communication and collaboration skills.

Preferred Qualifications:
Certification in HR analytics (e.g., SHRM, HRCI, or People Analytics).

Experience with SQL or other database querying languages.

Knowledge of predictive analytics in HR.
